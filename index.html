ABOUT:

My name is Nicole Schiller. I earned my B.S. in Biology from the University of Minnesota in May 2024, and my Master of Geographic Information Science from the University of Minnesota in May 2026.

I am a Geographic Information Scientist and Cartographer based in Saint Paul, Minnesota. I am passionate about remote sensing, cartography, and spatial analysis for polar & Earth science applications.

I believe maps are for everyone, whether they realize it yet or not.

From rock and ice climbing to kayaking and hiking, I enjoy spending time in nature. My connection to art and science, fostered from childhood, has shaped my appreciation for GIS as an intersection of the two.

Ross Island and Beaufort Island

This map was produced for the Polar Geospatial Center at the University of Minnesota. It is a general reference map of Ross Island, with an inset of nearby Beaufort Island. The style of this map is inspired by the United States Geological Survey (USGS) 1:250,000-scale topographic reconnaissance maps published in the 1960s through the 1980s. These maps were often characterized by their generalized linework, minimal use of color, stunning shaded relief, contours, and a utilitarian sheet layout.

2025 NACIS Student Map and Poster Competition Best Design winner.
2025 UMN U-Spatial Mapping Prize Best Cartography, Graduate Student winner.

King George Island

This map was produced for the Polar Geospatial Center at the University of Minnesota. It is a satellite imagery general reference map of King George Island, Antarctica. King George Island is home to a plethora of international Antarctic facilities. Part of the Islands of the Antarctic Peninsula series, updated from a 2012 map series that utilized commerical satellite imagery.

James Ross Island

Additional satellite imagery general reference map in the Islands of the Antarctic Peninsula Series, produced for the Polar Geospatial Center at the University of Minnesota. Beautiful, swirling sea ice can be seen around James Ross Island in this late austral summer image.

Livingston Island

The third and final installment in the Islands of the Antarctic Peninsula series, produced for the Polar Geospatial Center at the University of Minnesota. This satellite imagery general reference map includes the famous C-shaped island, Deception Island.

Hennepin County Extreme Heat Risk Infographic

This infographic was created for a project through the University of Minnesota’s Resilient Communities Project that partnered MGIS students and Hennepin County. It depicts the results of this project, which aimed to create a baseline heat risk assessment, including public health indicators and impacts, and refine the County's climate vulnerability index.

McMurdo Dry Valleys - ASMA No. 2

Stark general reference map of the McMurdo Dry Valleys of Antarctica, an incredibly important location for science. This map utilizes varied imagery saturation inside versus outside of the ASMA boundary and stark shadows that extend past the map frame. Produced for the Polar Geospatial Center at the University of Minnesota.

2026 UMN U-Spatial Mapping Prize Best Cartography, Graduate Student winner.
